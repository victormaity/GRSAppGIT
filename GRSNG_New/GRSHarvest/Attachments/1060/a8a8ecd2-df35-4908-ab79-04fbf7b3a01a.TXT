study</Timeframe></Measure></MeasuresPrimary><MeasureSecondary></MeasureSecondary></MeasuresOutcome><ProtocolAndOutcomes><AimsAndScope>&lt;AimAndScope&gt;&lt;para&gt;The purpose of the study was to evaluate the carcinogens in lung tissue from smokers  and non-smokers with newly diagnosed stage I, stage II, or stage III      non-small cell lung cancer. &lt;br/&gt;Objectives of the study were to:&lt;br/&gt;Assess lung tissue from patients with stage I, stage II, stage IIIA, or stage IIIB non-small cell lung cancer for specific tobacco smoke carcinogens (including polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons [PAH] and DNA adducts, alterations in specific genes, including p53 and K-ras, and expression of HER2 and estrogen receptors alpha and beta).&lt;br/&gt;Determine whether tobacco smoke carcinogens differ by gender and smoking status, adjusting for potential exposures and influential factors, including family smoking status, medication use, and hormonal and reproductive factors.&lt;br/&gt;Measure the levels of PAH-DNA adducts in lymphocytes and lung tissue and examine correlations between the two tissue sources.&lt;br/&gt;Determine whether DNA damage levels in tissue as well as in lymphocytes are higher in females than in males for the same level of smoking.&lt;br/&gt;Determine polymorphisms in several genes involved in the metabolism of the specific carcinogens investigated and in steroidogenesis and metabolism.&lt;br/&gt;Summarize patient self-report questionnaire data on active and passive smoking, other carcinogenic exposures, smoking preference, economic and educational status, family smoking status, reproductive factors, weight loss, and medication use by categories of male versus female and never-smoker versus ever-smoker.&lt;/para&gt;&lt;/AimAndScope&gt;</AimsAndScope><ProtocolDescription>&lt;ProtocolDescription&gt;&lt;para&gt;This would be a case-series, multicenter study. Patients would be stratified according to      gender and smoking status (never smoker [&amp;lt; 100 cigarettes smoked during lifetime] vs ever      smoker [&gt;/=  100 cigarettes smoked during lifetime]). Patients would complete the Lung Cancer Epidemiology Questionnaire for detailed assessment of the      following:&lt;br/&gt;Exposure to active and passive smoke.&lt;br/&gt;Occupational exposures.&lt;br/&gt;Reproductive and hormonal risk factors.&lt;br/&gt;Weight loss.&lt;br/&gt;Economic and educational status.&lt;br/&gt;Family smoking statu.s&lt;br/&gt;Medication use.&lt;br/&gt;Other variables relevant for the analysis (eg, HER2, estrogen receptor status)          , peripheral blood samples would be collected for research studies. Previously collected           tissue samples would  also be studied in the laboratory. Samples would be examined for DNA adduct           levels. Estrogen receptor alpha and beta would be assessed by immunohistochemistry (IHC). HER2           expression and amplification would be measured by chromogenic in situ hybridization. IHC and           DNA-polymerase chain reaction (PCR)-single-stranded conformational polymorphism assay           would be used to analyze p53 mutations. RAS mutations would be analyzed with restriction fragment           length polymorphism-PCR assay. Polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons and 4-aminobiphenyl-DNA           damage would be assessed by IHC and immunofluorescence. Matrix-assisted laser           desorption/ionization time of flight mass spectrometry would be used to genotype           polymorphisms, including CYP1A1, CYP1B1, GSTM1, GSTP1, MPO, NAT-1, NAT-2, CYP19, CYP17,           SULT1A1. Patients would be followed annually for up to five years. A total of patients (n = 900) would be accrued for this study (female and male      smoker strata closed to accrual as of 7/15/07).&lt;/para&gt;&lt;/ProtocolDescription&gt;</ProtocolDescription></ProtocolAndOutcomes><AdverseEventsByInterventions></AdverseEventsByInterventions><DoseRouteByInterventions></DoseRouteByInterventions><SitesByCountries><SitesByCountry
